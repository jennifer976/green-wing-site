### The 7-Day Market Radar: The April 1st "Sting"

If you haven’t looked at your projected fixed costs for the coming quarter, now is the time to sit down (and maybe grab a coffee).

We are currently tracking a projected **60% spike in TNUoS (Transmission Network Use of System) and other fixed energy costs** set to land on April 1st. While the headlines often focus on wholesale gas prices, these "non-commodity" costs are the silent margin-killers for hospitality and retail businesses.

**What’s happening?** The UK’s National Grid is undergoing a massive transformation to support electrification and renewable input. To pay for this, the charges for using the network are being recalibrated. For businesses with high peak-time usage: think commercial kitchens at 7:00 PM or retail malls during Saturday surges: these hikes are going to be felt immediately.

**The Business Impact:** For a mid-sized hotel, a 60% increase in these specific transmission and distribution elements could add thousands to the annual bill without a single extra lightbulb being turned on. It changes the ROI calculation for almost every efficiency project you have on the table.

**The Proactive Move:** Don't wait for the bill to arrive in May to start complaining. Now is the window to look at "load shifting." If you can move some of your energy-intensive processes away from the peak TRIAD periods, you can mitigate a significant portion of these hikes. This is exactly where an **energy efficiency consultant** becomes an investment rather than a cost. We help you map out exactly when you're pulling from the grid and how to flatten that curve before the April sting hits.

### Found on the Floor: The Mystery of the "Vampire Wings"

One of the best parts of our job is getting out on-site. Last week, while conducting a **commercial energy audit** for a multi-site property developer, we stumbled across what we call a "Vampire Wing."

We were looking at a large mixed-use building that was roughly 30% vacant due to a recent tenant transition. On paper, the energy bills should have dropped significantly. In reality? They had barely budged.

**The Discovery:** When we got into the plant room and checked the HVAC (Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning) settings, we found that the building's management system (BMS) was still treating the vacant floors as if they were fully occupied.



* Heating was pumping at 21°C in empty corridors.
* The lighting sensors were set to "Always On" in the basement storage areas.
* Ventilation fans were running at 100% capacity 24/7.



**The Lesson:** We call these "vampire loads": energy consumption that drains your bank account while providing zero operational value. In this specific case, the building was "bleeding" nearly £1,200 a month on floors where nobody was even breathing.

**How to Fix It:** If you haven’t had a physical walkthrough of your assets lately, you are likely paying for energy you don't use. A robust **energy monitoring system** can flag these anomalies instantly by showing you real-time data visualisations of consumption by zone. If your "empty" floor looks like a busy office on your dashboard, you know exactly where to send the maintenance team.

You can learn more about how we identify these gaps on our [Solutions page](https://greenwingenergysolutions.com/solutions).

### The Net Zero Quick-Fix: Plug-and-Play Solar

For a long time, solar power was seen as a "big player" game. If you didn't own the roof or couldn't commit to a 20-year structural overhaul, you were locked out.

That changed this week. The UK government has signalled a major push for **"plug-in solar" panels** specifically aimed at small businesses and the retail sector.

**Why this matters for Retail:** If you run a high-street shop or a small boutique hotel, you might not have the capital (or the landlord's permission) for a full-scale roof array. Plug-in solar kits are designed to be much more flexible. Under new permitted development rules, the red tape is being cut back, allowing businesses to install smaller-scale, modular systems that can be plugged directly into the existing internal electrical system.

**The Quick-Fix Stats:**



* **Installation Time:**

   Hours, not weeks.
* **Portability:**

   If you move premises, you can often take the kit with you.
* **Impact:**

   While it won’t power a whole factory, it can easily offset the daily running costs of your POS systems, lighting, and small appliances.



It’s a low-friction entry point into the world of renewables. It sends a clear message to your customers that you’re serious about sustainability, without requiring a six-figure CAPEX budget.

### Legislative Loophole: The CMA is Watching Your Fuel

If your business involves a fleet: whether it’s delivery vans for a retail chain or maintenance vehicles for a property firm: this one is for you.

The Competition and Markets Authority (CMA) has just announced a massive transparency crackdown on fuel pricing. For years, there has been a "transparency gap" between wholesale cost drops and the prices we see at the pump. The CMA is now pushing for a mandatory open-data scheme where retailers must share their prices in real-time.

**The Opportunity:** This isn't just about saving a few pennies at the pump. For businesses, this opens the door for new fuel-tracking software and better route planning based on real-time cost data.

**The Straightforward Take:** Don't just accept your monthly fuel card statement as "the cost of doing business." As transparency increases, the variance between providers will widen. Use this new data to negotiate better rates with your suppliers or to switch your fleet's refuelling habits. In a world of tight margins, being the first to leverage legislative shifts like this is how you stay ahead.
