Most businesses focus on energy prices.

They spend weeks: sometimes months: haggling over unit rates, switching suppliers, and obsessing over wholesale market fluctuations. It makes sense on the surface. If you can shave half a penny off your kWh rate, you’ve won, right?

Not necessarily.

In our experience at Green Wing Energy Solutions, we see a recurring pattern: companies are winning the battle on price but losing the war on volume. In reality, a large portion of energy spend is not driven by the price on the contract at all. It is driven by how energy is being used (or wasted) inside the building.

We call this the "Invisible Overhead." It is the energy you pay for that delivers absolutely zero value to your operations, your staff, or your customers.

### The hidden issue: energy that delivers no value

Recently, we conducted a review for a client with a significant multi-site property portfolio. One specific building stood out. Due to shifts in their operational model, roughly 30% of the floor space was vacant.

Logically, you would expect the energy bill for that site to drop by a comparable margin. If 30% of the building isn’t being used, 30% of the energy shouldn’t be consumed.

But when we looked at the data, the needle had barely moved. On paper, the building was still operating as if it were at 100% capacity.

Why? Because the building’s systems didn’t know the business had changed.

We found heating systems firing up at 6:00 AM in empty wings. Ventilation was operating at full industrial capacity in storage areas that hadn't seen a footfall in weeks. Lighting was set to “always on” in corridors leading to nowhere.

There were no faults. No alarms were triggered. The Building Management System (BMS) wasn't "broken." It was simply doing exactly what it was originally programmed to do five years ago.

The result? This business was spending over £1,000 per month on a completely unused space. Over a year, that is £12,000 of pure profit leaking out of a single site through the ceiling tiles.

### Why this happens: The "Set and Forget" Trap

Most commercial buildings are set up once: during the initial fit-out or the last major refurbishment: and then they are left to run.

The problem is that businesses are dynamic, but building settings are static. As occupancy changes, operations shift, or sites evolve, energy settings rarely get revisited. Facilities teams are often stretched thin, focusing on reactive maintenance and "keeping the lights on." They rarely have the bandwidth to ask if the lights *should* be on in the first place.

Over time, this leads to "Operational Drift." Without intervention, businesses quietly start paying for:



* **Empty Space:**

   Conditioned air and light for nobody.
* **Idle Equipment:**

   Servers, printers, and industrial machinery drawing power in standby modes that aren't truly "off."
* **System Over-Performance:**

   HVAC systems running at 100% capacity when 40% would suffice for the current head-count.

### The simple question worth asking

If you are a Director or a Facilities Manager, there is one question that is worth more than a dozen price-comparison spreadsheets:

**"If part of our site was empty today… would our energy bill actually go down?"**

If the answer is "I’m not sure" or a flat "No," then there is a massive opportunity sitting right under your nose.

For multi-site operators, this uncertainty is multiplied. If you have 20 sites and each is wasting just £500 a month on "ghost energy," that is £120,000 a year stripped from your bottom line. That is a significant sum that could be reinvested into [solutions](https://greenwingenergysolutions.com/solutions) that actually drive growth, rather than just heating an empty hallway.
